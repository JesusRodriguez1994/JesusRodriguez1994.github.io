Early Life:

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American born on July 10, 1856. Tesla was born and raised in the Austrian Empire.Tesla was fathered by Milutin Tesla an Eastern Orthodox priest and mother was Duka Tesla. Tesla was the fourth of five children, he had three sisters, Milka, Angelina and Marica and an older brother named Dane. Tesla had an education in engineering and physics.Tesla had interest's in electricity due to his physics professor. Tesla father wanted him to become a priest. When Tesla emigrated to the United States, in the early 1880s, he worked for Edison Machine Works in New York City.

Contribution:

After working with Edison in 1885 they parted ways. Tesla received funding for Tesla Electric Light Company. Tesla invented AC electrical system, however, it was not successful until 1887. Tesla invention was caught by a businessman George Westinghouse. George was seeking for a solution to supply the nation with long-distance power. George purchased his patens for $6,000 dollars and stock in Westinghouse Corporation. However, Edison arranged negative campaigns against Tesla alternate current system. Edison also created the Tesla coil in which laid the foundation for wireless technologies. Tesla AC invention took off when Westinghouse Corporation was chosen to supply lighting at 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago. Tesla designed the first AC hydroelectric power plants in the United States in Niagara Falls. AC power became the preeminent power system of the 20th century and currently remains the worldwide standard. Tesla also pioneered in other technologies such as radar technology, X-ray technology, remote control and rotating magnetic field.

Legacy & Fall:

Tesla grew an obsession with wireless transmission of energy in the 1900s. Tesla wanted to build a global wireless communications system to be transmitted from a large electrical tower. Tesla wanted to provide free electricity throughout the world. His inventors including J.P Morgan cut Tesla funding leaving Tesla with no funding support. Tesla had to abandon his projects and eventually declaring bankruptcy. Tesla suffered a nervous breakdown and worked last days a consultant. Tesla then spent most of his time feeding pigeons in New York City park. Tesla died on January 7, 1943, at the age of 86.
